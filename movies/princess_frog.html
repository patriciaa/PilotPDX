The Princess and the Frog

A fairy tale set in Jazz Age-era New Orleans and centered on a young woman named Tiana and her fateful kiss with a frog prince who desperately wants to be human again. (imdb.com)

Role of Women (6)

When Tiana was a young girl, her father would tell her how good food brought people together. Her father worked long hours at the mill to provide for his family, but had a different dream; to open up a restaurant. When her father died before reaching that dream, Tiana set on with his example: work hard to get what you want. On the contrary, Charlotte La Bouff, Tiana’s friend is from a wealthy family and has never had to work.

Portrayal of LGBT Characters (NR)

No Representation

Overall Representation (7)

This, like most Disney movies, is a good family movie. There are traces of misrepresentaions, however.

Portrayal of Minorities and People of Color(8)

It is clear who the “minority” is if Prince Naveen were to compare himself to Tiana. Although Naveen is disinherited by his parents (until he marries), he has no care in the world.
